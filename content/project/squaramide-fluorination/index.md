Squaramides are a versatile class of compounds with wide-ranging applications: they serve as bioisosteres in Medicinal Chemistry, as ligands in coordination chemistry, and as powerful hydrogen-bond donors in Organic Synthesis. Their two NH groups are capable of effectively donating hydrogen atoms and stabilizing anions — including fluoride — through hydrogen-bonding interactions.

This property was shown to be useful for enabling fluorination of alkyl halides via Sn2 processes by enhancing the solubility of fluoride anions in solution. Building on these foundations, this project aims to systematically describe how different stereoelectronic environments around the squaramide scaffold impact its hydrogen-bond donor capabilities and, consequently, its effectiveness as a fluorination enabler.
