abstract: The free energy profile of the piperidine catalyzed Knoevenagel condensation
  reaction of acetylacetone with benzaldehyde has been obtained by theoretical calculations.
  The carbinolamine formation step involves catalysis by methanol solvent, and its
  decomposition takes place via hydroxide ion elimination without a classical transition
  state, leading to the iminium ion. Hydroxide ion deprotonates the acetylacetone,
  forming an enolate that attacks the iminium ion and leads to an addition intermediate.
  The final step involves elimination of piperidine catalyst. Our analysis suggests
  the iminium ion formation has the highest barrier and the catalytic effect of piperidine
  is facilitating the elimination step rather than activation of the benzaldehyde
  electrophile. Experimental measures of the kinetics lead to an observed free energy
  barrier of 20.0 kcal mol--1, in good agreement with the theoretical value of 21.8
  kcal mol--1 based on the free energy profile.
