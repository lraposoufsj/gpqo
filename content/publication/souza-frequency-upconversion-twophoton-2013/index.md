abstract: Two-photon absorption and two-photon excitation fluorescence of salicylaldehyde
  azine 1 crystals were investigated. It was observed an intense visible fluorescence
  when this material was excited with a laser tuned at the near infrared region. Varying
  the laser intensity we characterized this phenomenon as a simultaneous two-photon
  laser absorption process. Using open aperture Z-scan measurements we characterized
  this two-photon absorption phenomenon and measured the value of the two-photon absorption
  cross-section of this molecule to be equal to 87GM. Our results indicate that this
  is a promising organic material aiming nonlinear photonics applications.
