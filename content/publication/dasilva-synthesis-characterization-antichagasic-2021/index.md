abstract: Chagas disease is a neglected disease, being one of the leading causes of
  death from infectious diseases. In view of the severity of this pathology, this
  work describes the synthesis of new thiosemicarbazones derived from chalcones and
  dibenzalacetones as potential drugs for the treatment of this disease. The structures
  of all compounds were elucidated by infrared (IR) and nuclear magnetic resonance
  (1H and 13C NMR) spectroscopies. The chalcone derived thiosemicarbazones 10-14 were
  tested against the intracellular amastigote form of the protozoan Trypanosoma cruzi
  and had their cytotoxicity assessed using LLC-MK2 cells. The compound 10 (IC50~=~12.25
  textmu M) presented the best activity when compared with the standard drug benznidazole
  (IC50~=~5.64 textmu M).
